# LinkedIn Post: Agent Advantage Conference Takeaways

## Post Text (LinkedIn-native — copy-paste directly)

I attended Google Cloud's "Agent Advantage" event last week in Sydney.

3 hours of demos, architecture deep dives, and real-world case studies on building AI agents at scale.

Here are the 5 things that stuck with me 👇

1️⃣ Only 12% of agent projects make it to production.

That stat hit hard. We're all building POCs and demos — but the gap between "impressive demo" and "production system that delivers business value" is enormous. The teams that close it aren't the most technical. They're the ones where business and engineering speak the same language about what agents can and can't do.

2️⃣ MCP is the "USB-C of agents."

Love this analogy from the talk. Model Context Protocol is becoming the universal standard for how agents connect to data and tools. Instead of writing custom integrations for every system, you write to one protocol. The industry — including Google — has actually aligned on a single standard. That almost never happens.

3️⃣ The real competitive moat isn't models or infrastructure.

It's your proprietary data + the agents you build on top of it. Foundation models are commoditising. Cloud platforms are commoditising. What isn't commoditising: your customer data, your domain knowledge, your operational workflows. That's where the IP lives.

4️⃣ Agent architecture is splitting into 3 clear patterns:

→ Function calling (model + tools, simplest pattern)
→ Agent-to-data via MCP (agent connects to databases, APIs, enterprise systems)
→ Agent-to-agent orchestration (mesh of specialised agents coordinating on complex tasks)

Most teams are still on pattern 1. The teams seeing production results are moving to pattern 2. Pattern 3 is where the industry is heading.

5️⃣ The "scale paradox" is real.

2025 was the adoption year — getting employees fluent, running pilots. 2026 is supposed to be the exponential growth year. But scaling agents is fundamentally different from scaling traditional software. You need sandboxed execution, memory management, evaluation frameworks, and governance — all of which are still being figured out.

The best demo of the day: a voice agent that handled multilingual car sales (switching between English and Spanish mid-conversation), used the camera to identify a notebook colour, and filtered search results based on it. All running on Gemini with MCP tool integrations.

The gap between what's possible in a demo and what's running in production is still wide. But the platform layer is maturing fast.
